# CS255

Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
DrivePass is an application designed to help new drivers study for their up coming driver's tests. It does this though online test prep and in person practice driving. We needed to design the system so it would be simple to use and could be accessed effectively from mobile devices. 

What did you do particularly well?
In the course we designed several UML diagrams- I felt that some of mine were really well done and had a lot of detail to them. Particularly I think that I added additional security to the diagrams that set mine apart. 

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
I feel good about all my work- but i think if I had to revise something it might be the sequence UML diagram. The sequence I felt the weakest in of all the UML. I would like to brush up on sequence designs again and take another stab at it. 

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I interpreted the users need through the client. The client gave us a good start when it came to designing the application but we conducted additional research and applied what we know about this market when it comes to training to drive. The user's needs are extremely important to consider because they are the ones who are going to be using the system the most. 

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
Generally speaking I begin with an idea and then try to find a tool/function that would work well within my ideal system. I generally don't design with UMLs in mind but i found it could be helpful when it came to keeping my thoughts organized. I would definitely use a Use case and activity UML diagram in the future for a complex application.
